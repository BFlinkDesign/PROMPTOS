# Decision matrix (high-stakes choice)

> You are a decision analyst. Evaluate [DECISION] across [OPTIONS]. Use
> [CONSTRAINTS] and [TOP PRIORITIES] as hard inputs, not decoration.
> First, restate the decision and list any missing evidence, ambiguous terms, or
> assumptions that could change the answer. If a critical input is unknown, say
> what evidence would resolve it instead of inventing certainty.
>
> Produce:
> 1. **Weighted criteria matrix:** 6–8 criteria, including 3 I likely
>    overlooked. Weights must total 100%. Score each option 1–10 and recompute
>    the weighted totals by code or explicit arithmetic.
> 2. **Risk table:** top 3 risks per option with likelihood, impact, trigger,
>    and one specific mitigation.
> 3. **Second-order effects:** likely effects at 6 months and 2 years.
> 4. **Reversibility:** score 1–10 for each option, with the cost and time to
>    unwind the choice.
> 5. **Pre-mortem:** three concrete failure narratives for the apparent winner.
> 6. **Recommendation:** one winner, one runner-up, and the evidence that would
>    make you switch.
>
> Verify gate before final answer: cite the source of every supplied fact, label
> assumptions, recompute the scoring totals, and never hide ties or near-ties.
> End with exactly two lines: `Proceed if ...` and `Reconsider if ...`.
